Transforming Perspectives on Neurodiversity

With Awareness, Education, and Empowerment.

AROUND ONE IN FIVE PEOPLE ARE
NEURODIVERGENT.

“Neurodiversity may be every bit as crucial for the human race as biodiversity is for life in general. Who can say what form of wiring will prove best at any given moment?”

— Harvey Blume, The Atlantic, 1998

Although there is broad diversity across the population, some individuals have neurological variations that make it particularly challenging for their communication, self-expression, and interactions with others. Neurodivergence is a broad umbrella and is not just autism. Neurological variations can include autism, ADHD, dyslexia, dyspraxia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, and tics. (However, Judy Singer, who coined the term, proposes that we are ALL neurodiverse because no two humans on the planet are exactly the same.)

The environments within which individuals with these neurovariations learn, work, and live can either facilitate or inhibit their growth and development.

The purpose of this initiative is to create environments that will maximally facilitate these individuals to grow and achieve their full potential. It is about facilitating a community of practice for universities, colleges, employers, and service providers to work together to create these environments and opportunities for neurodivergent young adults.

The Resources section includes a vast array of resources that have been created or curated for use by neurodivergent students, their parents and carers, employers, and universities.

WHY THE NeuroNurture?

THE CHALLENGE

There are countless neurodivergent individuals who possess incredible talents and unique perspectives.

Unfortunately, many of these individuals face significant barriers in accessing education tailored to their needs. These challenges may include:

  • Lack of inclusive educational environments that accommodate different learning styles and needs.
  • Inadequate support systems in traditional educational settings, which can lead to frustration, underachievement, or dropping out.
  • Limited access to specialized resources such as assistive technologies, tailored curricula, or trained educators who understand the diverse ways in which neurodivergent individuals learn.

Without proper support, neurodivergent individuals may struggle to fully participate in educational opportunities, preventing them from realizing their full potential.

THE GOAL

The aim of the NeuroNurture is to:

  • Provide tailored educational programs and resources that cater specifically to the needs of neurodivergent learners, ensuring they receive a quality education that respects and leverages their unique strengths.
  • Create an inclusive and supportive learning environment where neurodivergent individuals feel safe, understood, and empowered to pursue their educational goals.
  • Offer access to assistive technologies, flexible learning arrangements, and trained educators who are skilled in adapting teaching methods to support neurodivergent students.
  • Promote awareness and understanding among educators and peers about neurodiversity, fostering a more inclusive and empathetic educational community.
  • Establish partnerships with educational institutions worldwide to create a network of Neurodiversity Education Hubs, ensuring widespread access to specialized education for neurodivergent individuals.

By addressing these challenges and working towards these goals, the Neurodiversity Education Hub aims to ensure that all neurodivergent individuals have access to an education that empowers them to thrive and succeed.
